Where do you go after your first pieces? This ladder lays out a long-term route through the piano
repertoire, one rung at a time — from beginner tunes to advanced Romantic writing — so you always know
what to reach for next.

## Outcomes
- See the whole beginner-to-advanced arc at a glance
- Choose a well-graded next piece at every stage
- Cover Baroque, Classical and Romantic styles

## Section: Grade 1–2 — Beginnings
- beethoven-ode-to-joy (note: Your very first melody.)
- bach-minuet-in-g

## Section: Grade 3–4 — Building
- clementi-sonatina-op36-no1-1st-mvt (note: First Classical form.)
- schumann-melody-op68-no1
- burgmuller-arabesque-op100-no2 (note: A brilliant little étude.)

## Section: Grade 5–6 — Intermediate
- bach-invention-no1-bwv772 (note: Two-part counterpoint.)
- chopin-prelude-e-minor-op28-no4 (note: First Romantic voicing.)

## Section: Grade 7–9 — Advanced
- chopin-nocturne-op9-no2 (note: The nocturne touch. skills: [rubato])
- debussy-clair-de-lune (note: Colour and pedal artistry. skills: [pedaling])
- beethoven-moonlight-sonata-1st-mvt (note: Sustained control and atmosphere. skills: [pedaling, voicing])
