Folk songs are where music lives in everyday life. These public-domain melodies are simple to learn,
endlessly arrangeable, and instantly recognisable — perfect for playing, singing, and sharing.

## Outcomes
- Build a repertoire of songs you can play from memory
- Practise phrasing on simple, singable melodies
- Accompany a melody with basic chords

## Section: Ballads & Airs
- greensleeves-trad (note: The most famous English air — mind the minor/major shifts.)
- danny-boy (note: A soaring melody; save your breath for the climax.)
- scarborough-fair (note: A modal classic in Dorian.)
- shenandoah (note: A wide, rolling American ballad.)

## Section: Hymns & Spirituals
- amazing-grace-trad (note: A pentatonic melody — playable in one position.)
- simple-gifts (note: The Shaker tune Copland made famous.)
- swing-low-sweet-chariot

## Section: Sing-Alongs
- house-of-the-rising-sun (note: A brooding minor classic.)
- when-the-saints-go-marching-in (note: Bright and easy — great for a first jam.)
