A gentle, motivating on-ramp for the brand-new pianist. Each piece adds one new idea — a hand shift,
a two-hand texture, a longer phrase — so you build real skills while playing music you'll recognise.
Work through them in order and revisit the early ones as warm-ups.

## Outcomes
- Play ten complete pieces with steady time
- Read simple treble and bass staves fluently
- Coordinate the hands in short two-part textures
- Build a repeatable daily practice habit

## Section: First Notes
Single-line and one-hand-friendly tunes to build confidence at the keys.
- beethoven-ode-to-joy (note: Start hands-separate and slow; feel the four-bar phrases. skills: [reading, phrasing])
- bach-minuet-in-g (note: Keep the left hand light under the melody.)

## Section: Two Hands Together
Pieces that ask the hands to cooperate — the real beginning of piano playing.
- burgmuller-la-candeur-op100-no1 (note: Let the melody sing over an even accompaniment. skills: [voicing])
- schumann-melody-op68-no1 (note: Aim for a smooth, connected right-hand line.)
- czerny-op-599-no-1 (note: A tiny study — use it as a daily warm-up.)

## Section: First Milestones
Short, complete pieces that feel like an achievement.
- clementi-sonatina-op36-no1-1st-mvt (note: Your first taste of classical form.)
- beethoven-fur-elise-opening (note: The famous opening — mind the pedal changes. skills: [pedaling])
