Ragtime pairs a steady march-like left hand with a syncopated right — the sound that became jazz. This
anthology starts with the gentler rags and works up to the classics, all drawn from the public domain.

## Outcomes
- Keep a steady stride bass under a syncopated melody
- Read and voice dense chordal writing
- Play a complete Joplin rag up to tempo

## Section: Gentle Rags
- joplin-solace (note: A slow "Mexican serenade" — start here for the feel. skills: [syncopation])
- joplin-the-entertainer (note: The famous one; hands-separate first. skills: [stride])

## Section: The Classics
- joplin-maple-leaf-rag (note: The rag that started it all — take the bass slowly. skills: [stride, articulation])
- joplin-pineapple-rag
- joplin-ragtime-dance

## Section: Beyond Joplin
- lamb-american-beauty-rag (note: Joseph Lamb's lush harmonies stretch the style.)
- scott-frog-legs-rag (note: James Scott's brilliant, virtuosic writing.)
