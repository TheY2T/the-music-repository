Improvising isn't magic — it's a small kit of scales and chord shapes you learn to hear and reach for.
This path builds that kit, then puts it straight over real tunes. Keep the
[Improvise guide](/tools/improvise) open alongside it to see which scales fit each chord, and jam over
the changes with the [progression generator](/tools/progression-generator).

## Outcomes
- Play the minor pentatonic and blues scales in a usable position
- Understand the chords a solo is moving over
- Solo over a 12-bar blues with confidence

## Section: The scales you'll reach for
- minor-pentatonic-scale-shapes (note: The five-note scale behind most rock and blues solos. skills: [scales])
- blues-scale-piano (note: Add the "blue note" and you have the blues scale.)
- omt-modes-of-major (note: When you want more colours than the pentatonic. skills: [analysis])

## Section: The chords underneath
- omt-seventh-chords (note: Blues and jazz live on seventh chords — know what you're blowing over.)
- 12-bar-blues-in-a (note: The form you'll solo over most.)

## Section: Put it over a tune
- handy-st-louis-blues (note: A classic to trade solos on.)
- frankie-and-johnny (note: A 12-bar blues ballad — swap the melody for a solo.)
