Sonatinas are how generations of pianists learned Classical style — clean articulation, balanced phrases,
and the architecture of sonata form in miniature. This graded set builds from Clementi to Mozart.

## Outcomes
- Play clean two-hand Classical textures
- Understand exposition, development and recapitulation
- Apply scale and arpeggio technique in real repertoire

## Section: Grade 3 — First Sonatinas
- clementi-sonatina-op36-no1-1st-mvt (note: The classic first sonatina; crisp Alberti bass. skills: [articulation])
- beethoven-sonatina-in-g-anh5 (note: Elegant and tuneful — attributed to the young Beethoven.)

## Section: Grade 4–5 — Building Fluency
- kuhlau-sonatina-op20-no1-1st-mvt (note: Brilliant passagework — practise the scales slowly. skills: [scales])

## Section: Grade 6+ — Toward the Sonata
- mozart-sonata-k545-1st-mvt (note: "Sonata facile" — famously not so easy. Aim for evenness. skills: [scales, phrasing])
