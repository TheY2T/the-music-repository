## Goal

Carl Czerny — a pupil of Beethoven and teacher of Liszt — wrote his *Practical Method for Beginners*,
Op. 599, as a graded set of 100 short studies. **No. 1 is the very first**, and it does one job well:
it settles a beginner into a **five-finger position** and builds **evenness, legato, and a quiet
hand** before anything harder arrives. If your scales feel lumpy, this study smooths them out.

## How to do it

The piece stays in C major, in common time (`4/4`), with both hands anchored over the five white keys
`C D E F G`.

1. **Set the five-finger position:** right-hand thumb on `C`, finger 5 on `G`; left hand mirrors it an
   octave below (finger 5 on `C`, thumb on `G`). Fingers rest lightly on their keys — no reaching.
2. **Right hand carries the melody** in steady quarter notes, walking up and down the five-finger
   shape (`C D E F G` and back). Keep fingers `1 2 3 4 5` over their own keys the whole time.
3. **Left hand supports** with longer, held notes underneath — let them ring cleanly without thumping.
4. **Count out loud** "1 2 3 4" for every bar so the pulse never sags.
5. **Play legato:** each finger presses only as the previous one releases, so notes connect with no
   gap and no overlap. Aim for a gentle *Allegro*, but start far slower — around ♩ = 66.
6. Hands separately first, then hands together once each is secure.

## Common mistakes

- **Bumpy quarter notes.** Uneven volume usually means the hand is tense — lighten the touch and slow
  down.
- **Collapsing fingers.** Keep a rounded hand; a flattening 4th or 5th finger loses control.
- **Losing the pulse when hands combine.** Drop back to a tempo where both hands stay effortless.

## How to progress

Only nudge the tempo up once the study is even and relaxed, then move on to Op. 599 No. 2 and beyond.
Reinforce the five-finger control with [Hanon Exercise No. 1](/hanon-virtuoso-pianist-no-1), and take
the same steadiness into the [C Major Scale — Two Octaves](/c-major-scale-two-octaves). Practise to a
click from [/tools/metronome](/tools/metronome).
