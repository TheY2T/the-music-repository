## Goal

Once you can play C major, the goal is to play a major scale **starting on any of the twelve notes**.
Every major scale follows the same interval recipe — whole and half steps `W W H W W W H` — so what
changes from key to key is only the **fingering** and which black keys are involved. Learning them all
unlocks nearly every piece and warms the hand up completely.

## How to do it

**White-key group — C, G, D, A, E major.** Same fingering as C:

- **RH ascending:** `1 2 3 1 2 3 4` (5 on the top note)
- **LH ascending:** `5 4 3 2 1 3 2 1`

**F major** (one flat, `B♭`):

- **RH:** `1 2 3 4 1 2 3 4` — the thumb tucks under after finger 4 (on `B♭`).
- **LH:** `5 4 3 2 1 3 2 1`

**The flat/black-key scales (B♭, E♭, A♭, D♭, G♭) and B major** use custom fingerings, but they all
obey one rule that makes them learnable:

1. **Never put the thumb on a black key.** The thumb lives on white keys, so the black keys fall under
   fingers 2, 3, and 4.
2. Because of that rule, most flat scales *start* on a finger other than 1 in the right hand — e.g.
   **B♭ major RH begins on finger 4** (`B♭`=4 `C`=1 `D`=2 `E♭`=3 `F`=1 `G`=2 `A`=3 `B♭`=4).
3. Learn each hand **separately and slowly**, watching where the thumb crosses. A fingering chart is
   worth its weight here — memorise one scale per week.
4. Practise in **circle-of-fifths order** (C → G → D → A → E …) so each new key adds just one sharp.

Start at ♩ = 60, two notes per beat, one octave, then extend to two.

## Common mistakes

- **Improvising the thumb on a black key.** It feels fine slowly and collapses at speed — follow the
  rule.
- **Skipping hands-separate work** on the harder keys.
- **Always starting from C.** Rotate which key you practise first so no scale stays weak.

## How to progress

Add octaves and speed with [/tools/metronome](/tools/metronome), then add contrary motion and
parallel thirds. Use the [Circle of Fifths tool](/tools/circle-of-fifths) and
[reference](/circle-of-fifths-reference) to drill keys in order, and revisit
[C Major Scale — Two Octaves](/c-major-scale-two-octaves) as your fingering template.
