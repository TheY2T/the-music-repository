## Goal

Slap bass is the percussive, funky sound at the heart of funk, R&B, and countless pop grooves —
pioneered by players like Larry Graham and made iconic by Marcus Miller and Flea. It combines two
motions: the **thumb slap** (a low, punchy attack) and the **finger pop** (a bright, snapping high
note). Learning it adds rhythm, dynamics, and attitude to your playing.

## How to do it

Think of the plucking hand as a relaxed hammer pivoting from the wrist, not the whole arm.

1. **The thumb slap ("T").** Rotate the wrist so the side of your thumb (the knuckle joint, laid
   roughly parallel to the strings) strikes down onto a string — usually the low `E` or `A` — right
   over the end of the fingerboard. **Bounce off immediately** so the note rings; don't press in. Aim
   for a short, drum-like *thump*.
2. **The finger pop ("P").** Hook your index or middle finger *under* a higher string (usually `G` or
   `D`), pull it slightly up and out, and release so it **snaps back against the frets** with a bright
   click.
3. **Combine them.** The classic groove alternates thumb and pop: `T P T P`. A great first pattern is
   slap the root on the `E` string, then pop the octave on the `G` string.
4. **Add left-hand muting.** Lightly rest unused fingers on the strings so only the intended note
   sounds — clarity in slap comes as much from muting as from striking.
5. **Start slow with a metronome,** ♩ = 60. Play `T (root) — P (octave)` as steady quarter notes
   until each attack is even and clean.

## Common mistakes

- **Using arm force instead of wrist rotation.** Tension kills the bounce — keep the wrist loose.
- **Digging in on the slap.** Bounce off the string; pressing in deadens the note.
- **Muddy, ringing strings.** Weak muting is the number-one beginner problem — mute aggressively with
  both hands.
- **Chasing speed too early.** Groove and evenness first; tempo follows.

## How to progress

Once `T P` is comfortable, add hammer-ons, ghost notes (muted percussive hits), and double thumbing.
Build lines from the [Bass Major Scale Fingerings](/bass-major-scale-fingerings) and
[Root–Fifth Bass Patterns](/bass-root-fifth-patterns), then apply slap to
[Building a Blues Bass Line](/bass-blues-line-basics). Lock the groove with a
[metronome](/tools/metronome).
